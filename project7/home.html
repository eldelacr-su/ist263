TRENDS WE ARE LOVING RIGHT NOW

Today, sneaker collaborations have become a major force in the sneaker industry, with many brands releasing highly anticipated collaborations with designers, artists, and celebrities on a regular basis. These collaborations have become a way for brands to generate buzz and excitement around their releases, while also tapping into new audiences and expanding their reach. Some recent examples of highly successful sneaker collaborations include the Nike x Off-White "The Ten" collection, which featured ten iconic Nike silhouettes reimagined by fashion designer Virgil Abloh, and the Adidas x Kanye West Yeezy line, which has become one of the most popular and sought-after sneaker collaborations of all time. In addition to established designers and celebrities, brands are also collaborating with up-and-coming artists and creatives, offering them a platform to showcase their work and connect with fans in new ways. This has resulted in a diverse range of sneaker collaborations that offer unique and unexpected designs, from the colorful and whimsical collaborations between Adidas and designer Jeremy Scott, to the minimalist and understated collaborations between Converse and fashion label Comme des Garçons. Overall, sneaker collaborations have become a driving force in the sneaker industry, offering brands and partners an opportunity to create unique and innovative designs that capture the attention of sneaker enthusiasts around the world.

FEATURED TREND OF THE PAST

A popular sneaker trend from the 90s was the platform sneaker trend. Platform sneakers were characterized by their thick and chunky soles that added height and made a bold fashion statement. The trend was popularized by brands like Buffalo, Steve Madden, and Converse, among others. Platform sneakers were often worn with short skirts or dresses, flared jeans, and oversized jackets for a trendy and edgy look. The trend was embraced by many music icons of the time, such as the Spice Girls and Gwen Stefani, and it became a symbol of the era's rebellious and youthful spirit. Today, platform sneakers have also made a comeback, with many modern brands and designers incorporating this style into their collections. The trend continues to offer a fun and bold way to add height and personality to any outfit..
